## 🎯 COMMON INTERVIEW QUESTIONS & ANSWERS

### Q1: "Why did Walmart come out as financially stronger?"

**Answer**:
"Walmart scored higher on most dimensions: 6x larger revenue base, higher absolute profitability ($15.5B vs $4.1B net income), superior cash generation ($26.8B OCF), and better operational efficiency (8.4x inventory turnover). These reflect Walmart's scale advantage and operational excellence. However, Target has higher profit margins (3.8% vs 2.4%), showing good positioning in the mid-market segment. The recommendation depends on investor priorities—Walmart for financial strength and stability, Target for niche positioning."

### Q2: "What metric was most telling of company health?"

**Answer**:
"Operating cash flow—I think this is more important than net income because it shows real cash the company generates. Walmart's $26.8B vs Target's $6.2B OCF tells a clearer story about financial strength than net income alone. The OCF margin (4.1% vs 5.7%) also shows how efficiently each converts revenue to cash. This is especially critical for retail where inventory management drives working capital."

### Q3: "How did you validate your data?"

**Answer**:
"I sourced all data from SEC EDGAR, which publishes official audited financial statements. I cross-checked figures against investor relations websites and Yahoo Finance. I also validated reasonableness—for example, inventory turnover of 8.4x (every 43 days) makes sense for Walmart's business model. I standardized units (millions USD) and verified calculations using multiple approaches."

### Q4: "What would you do differently with more time?"

**Answer**:
"Several enhancements: (1) Extend to 10 years of data to see longer trends; (2) Include more retailers for benchmarking; (3) Incorporate stock performance and market data for investment returns analysis; (4) Create predictive model for future profitability; (5) Build dynamic dashboard that auto-updates from SEC API; (6) Add sensitivity analysis showing how changes in variables impact ratios."

### Q5: "What's the most important financial ratio you calculated?"

**Answer**:
"For retail, I'd say inventory turnover is most important—it directly impacts profitability, working capital, and competitive position. Walmart's 8.4x turnover is a key advantage: faster cash conversion, lower storage costs, less obsolescence risk. Combined with asset turnover (2.57x), it shows how efficiently Walmart converts company resources to sales. For most companies though, I'd prioritize ROE—it's the ultimate measure of shareholder value creation."
