# Financial Performance & Risk Analysis Report
## Walmart vs. Target: Comparative Financial Assessment

---

## Executive Summary

This report presents a comprehensive financial analysis comparing Walmart, Inc. and Target Corporation across five fiscal years (2020-2024). Through detailed examination of profitability, liquidity, solvency, operational efficiency, and cash flow metrics, this analysis evaluates which company demonstrates greater financial strength.

### Key Finding
**Walmart is the financially stronger company** based on:
- Superior revenue scale and absolute profitability
- Better operational efficiency in inventory management
- Stronger cash flow generation
- Greater financial flexibility

However, **Target remains financially sound** with sustainable profitability, adequate liquidity, and manageable debt levels.

## 6. Solvency & Financial Risk Analysis

### 6.1 Debt-to-Equity Ratio

| Year | Walmart | Target | Change |
|------|---------|--------|--------|
| 2024 | 1.83 | 1.58 | Walmart higher |
| 2023 | 1.84 | 1.54 | Walmart higher |
| 2020 | 1.79 | 1.62 | Walmart higher |

**Analysis:**
- Walmart carries more absolute debt but has assets to support it
- Target's lower leverage reflects smaller scale and less access to capital
- Both ratios are sustainable for large retailers
- Industry average for retail: 1.5-2.0

### 6.2 Debt Ratio (Debt as % of Assets)

| Year | Walmart | Target |
|------|---------|--------|
| 2024 | 64.7% | 61.2% |
| 2023 | 64.6% | 60.9% |

**Interpretation:**
- About 65% of Walmart's assets financed by debt (35% by equity)
- About 61% of Target's assets financed by debt (39% by equity)
- Both levels are appropriate for retail operations
- Target maintains slightly stronger equity cushion

### 6.3 Financial Stability Assessment

**Credit Strength:**
- Both companies maintain investment-grade credit ratings
- Strong operating cash flows support debt service
- Stable earnings enable consistent debt management
- Limited financial distress risk

**Debt Sustainability:**
```
Walmart: $163.3B total liabilities / $26.8B operating cash flow = 6.1x coverage
Target:  $38.5B total liabilities / $6.2B operating cash flow = 6.2x coverage
```
Both companies can service debt approximately 6 times over with operating cash flow.
